Advanced Mathematics

Old Subjects

  • Old Subjects

1. The real and complex number. T=5'5h + P=4'25h

1.1 Axiomatic of real numbers.
1.2. The body of complex numbers. Basic operations with complex numbers.
1.3. Geometric interpretation.
1.4. Euler's Formula. Exponentials and logarithms of complex numbers.

2. Metric and topological spaces. T=4h + P=3'5h

2.1. Definition of distance. Notable sets: balls, open, closed and environments.
2.2. Introduction to topological spaces. Notable sets. Notable points: interior, exterior, accumulation, adhesion, isolated.
2.3. Diameter and dimension of sets.

3. Limits and continuity. T=8h + P=7h

3.1. Successions and limits. Fundamental theorems. Cauchy successions.
3.2. Convergence criteria for successions of real numbers. Calculation of limits.
3.3. Numerical series. Convergence criteria.
3.4. Limits of functions. Infinite and infinite.
3.5. Continuity of functions. Theorems about continuity.

4. Differentiation in a variable. T=4h + P=3'5h

4.1. Review of the derivative concept and its geometric interpretation in a variable.
4.2. Taylor's formula for functions of a variable.
4.3. Concept of differential of a function in a point.

5. Differentiation in several variables. T=5'5h + P=4'25h

5.1. Partial derivatives. Directional derivatives. Relations with the concept of differential. Geometric interpretation of the differential and the partial derivatives of a function of several variables.
5.2. Jacobian Matrix. Relations between differentiability and partial derivatives.
5.3. Higher order partial derivatives.

6. Chain rule. Implicit function theorem. T=6h + P=4'5h

6.1. Chain rule. Application to the calculation of partial derivatives of composite functions.
6.2. The Theorem of Implicit Functions: Idea of Theorem, Statement and Applications.
6.3. Variable changes.

7. Taylor's formula and calculus of extremes in several variables. T=4h + P=3'5h

7.1. Taylor's formula for functions of several variables.
7.2. Relative extrema of a function. Necessary conditions. Hessian matrix and associated quadratic form.
7.3. Conditioned extremes. Rule of Lagrange multipliers. Applications.

8. Sequences and series of functions. T=4h + P=3'5h

8.1. Point and uniform convergence.
8.2. Power series. Radius convergence
8.3. Fourier series. Calculation of the Fourier series of some functions.
