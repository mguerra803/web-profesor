My name is Ángel Plaza and I am a professor at the University of Las Palmas de Gran Canaria. After 22 years working in the ULPGC, I have taught in the degrees of Architect, Telecommunications Engineer and Doctorate, Master's and Bachelor in Telecommunication Technologies. [See curriculum vitae].

Currently I teach Calculus and classes in the Master in Advanced Mathematics for Telecommunications Engineering at the IUMA (Instituto Universitario de Microelectrónica Aplicada).

Teaching has not been the only focus of my effort. I have also dedicated my knowledge to research, resulting in 2 national projects, more than 50 JCR publications, more than 80 participations in congresses and 3 directed doctoral theses. Also, I have been a member of the IMACS Technical Committee on Numerical Grid Generation since 2006, as well as a Reviewer of International Journals such as "Communications in Numerical Methods in Engineering" since 1996 or "Applied Mathematics and Computation" since 2009.

Abroad I have been able to work in the mobility program of the Texas Institute for Computational and Applied Mathematics (T.I.C.A.M.) and the Department of Computer Science (D.C.C.) of the University of Chile.
